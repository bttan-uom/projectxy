About Diabetes

What is diabetes? And what can I do to manage it?

What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a serious complex condition which can affect the entire body. Diabetes requires daily self care and if complications develop, diabetes can have a significant impact on quality of life and can reduce life expectancy. While there is currently no cure for diabetes, you can live an enjoyable life by learning about the condition and effectively managing it.

Symptoms

In type 1 diabetes, symptoms are often sudden and can be life-threatening; therefore it is usually diagnosed quite quickly. In type 2 diabetes, many people have no symptoms at all, while other signs can go unnoticed being seen as part of ‘getting older’.

Common symptoms include:

  • Being more thirsty than usual
  • Passing more urine
  • Feeling tired and lethargic
  • Always feeling hungry
  • Having cuts that heal slowly
  • Itching, skin infections
  • Blurred vision
  • Unexplained weight loss (type 1)
  • Gradually putting on weight (type 2)
  • Mood swings
  • Headaches
  • Feeling dizzy
  • Leg cramps

    Type 1 & Type 2

    Type 1 diabetes is thought to be caused by an autoimmune reaction (the body attacks itself by mistake) that stops your body from making insulin. Approximately 5-10% of the people who have diabetes have type 1. Symptoms of type 1 diabetes often develop quickly. If you have type 1 diabetes, you’ll need to take insulin every day to survive. Currently, no one knows how to prevent type 1 diabetes.

    With type 2 diabetes, your body doesn’t use insulin well and can’t manage blood sugar levels. About 90-95% of people with diabetes have type 2. It develops over many years and can be prevented or delayed with lifestyle changes, such as losing weight, eating healthy food, and being active.

    Synchronise your health information

    Regular checking and recording of your blood glucose level can help you make healthy lifestyle choices and can alert your clinician to a possible need for a change in how your diabetes is being managed. Effective management of diabetes means aiming for a careful balance between the foods you eat, how active you are and what medication you take.
